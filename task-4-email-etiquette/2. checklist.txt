Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

 ? DO's

 1. Keep your profiles professional.
 	Avoid posting your home address, phone number, college ID, or financial details on any public platform.
 2. Think before you post.
   	Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor or a future employer saw this?" If the answer is no, do not post it.
 3. Use privacy settings wisely. 
 	Regularly review who can see your posts, photos, and personal information. Keep personal accounts private if you wish to share casual content. 
 4. Give credit when sharing others' work.
 	Tag original creators and include sources when sharing articles, images, or ideas that are not your own.
 5. Engage positively and constructively.
 	Join academic groups, follow industry pages, and participate in meaningful discussions that help build your knowledge and network.
 6. Report harassment or harmful content.
  	Use the platform's built-in reporting tools if you encounter bullying, hate speech, or misinformation. Do not engage with or amplify harmful posts.

 ? DON'Ts

 1. Do not share personal information publicly.
  	Avoid posting your home address, phone number, college ID, or financial details on any public platform.
 2. Do not post or forward unverified news. 
	Spreading misinformation - even unintentionally - can have serious consequences. Always check a source before sharing
 3. Do not use social media to bully, mock, or demean others.
 	Cyberbullying is a punishable offence under Indian IT law. Treat others online exactly as you would in person. 
 4. Do not accept connection or friend requests from strangers.
	Unknown individuals requesting access to your profile may have malicious intent, including phishing or identity theft
 5. Do not post content while emotional or impulsive.
	Posts made in anger or frustration are often regretted. Draft, pause, and review before publishing anything sensitive.
