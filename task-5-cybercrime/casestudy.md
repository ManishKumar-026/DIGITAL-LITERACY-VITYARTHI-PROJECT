# Task 5 – Cybercrime Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

## Type of Cybercrime
**UPI/Online Payment Fraud** (also known as Digital Payment Scam)

---

## Case Study: The "Wrong Transfer" Trick

### What the Crime Is
UPI payment fraud is a form of financial cybercrime in which fraudsters manipulate victims into sending money or revealing sensitive credentials like UPI PINs, OTPs, or linked bank account details — ultimately resulting in unauthorised financial transactions.

### How It Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)

1. **Initial Contact:** A fraudster contacts a college student (usually via phone, WhatsApp, or OLX/Facebook Marketplace) claiming to have accidentally transferred money to the student's UPI ID.
2. **Building Trust:** They share a fake screenshot of a transaction showing money "sent" to the student's number.
3. **Creating Urgency:** The fraudster panics and pleads for an immediate refund, pressuring the student to act quickly.
4. **The Trap — Collect Request:** Instead of asking for a normal transfer, the fraudster sends a "Collect Request" on the victim's UPI app. The student, confused, enters their UPI PIN thinking they are "approving a refund."
5. **Money is Deducted:** Entering the PIN on a Collect Request does not receive money — it *sends* money. The student's account is debited.
6. **Disappearance:** The fraudster immediately becomes unreachable.

### Who is Usually Targeted
First-year college students, young adults using UPI apps for the first time, and individuals selling items online through platforms like OLX or Facebook Marketplace.

### Consequences
Victims lose money instantly — often between ₹2,000 and ₹50,000. Since UPI transactions are irreversible once processed, recovery is difficult. Emotional consequences include stress, shame, and distrust of digital payment systems. In some cases, students have lost money meant for semester fees or essential expenses.
