# Stay Safe Online – Cybercrime Prevention Checklist
### For College Students in India

---

## 🔐 General Online Safety

- [ ] **1. Use strong, unique passwords** for every account. A strong password has at least 8 characters including letters, numbers, and symbols. Never reuse the same password across multiple sites.

- [ ] **2. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** on your email, social media, and banking apps. This adds a second verification step even if your password is stolen.

- [ ] **3. Never click unknown links** in SMS, WhatsApp, or email messages — even if they appear to be from banks, TRAI, or government departments. These are common phishing attempts.

- [ ] **4. Verify the sender's identity** before responding to any message requesting personal information, OTPs, or account details.

- [ ] **5. Keep your apps and phone software updated.** Security patches in updates protect you from newly discovered vulnerabilities.

- [ ] **6. Avoid using public Wi-Fi** for banking, UPI transactions, or logging into sensitive accounts. Use your mobile data instead.

---

## 💸 Financial / UPI Safety (Mandatory)

- [ ] **7. Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or bank password** with anyone — not even someone claiming to be from your bank, Google Pay support, or a government office. These will NEVER be asked for legitimately.

- [ ] **8. Understand the difference between "Send" and "Collect" on UPI.** A Collect Request asks YOU to enter your PIN — which means money will LEAVE your account. If a stranger sends you a collect request claiming it is a "refund," do NOT enter your PIN. Reject the request immediately.

---

## 📱 Social Media & Identity Safety

- [ ] **9. Do not post Aadhaar card, PAN card, or bank details** online or share them via WhatsApp with unknown contacts.

- [ ] **10. Report cyberbullying, threatening messages, or impersonation immediately** — do not respond to the perpetrator. Take screenshots as evidence.

---

## 🚨 Reporting Channels

If you are a victim of any cybercrime:

| Channel | Details |
|---|---|
| **National Cyber Crime Portal** | 🌐 [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) — File a complaint online 24/7 |
| **Cyber Crime Helpline** | 📞 **1930** — Available 24x7 for financial fraud |
| **Local Police** | Visit the nearest police station and file an FIR |
| **Bank Helpline** | Call your bank immediately to block the account/card if financial fraud has occurred |

> ⚡ **Act fast.** If UPI fraud occurs, call **1930 within the first few hours** — quick reporting increases the chances of recovering your money.
